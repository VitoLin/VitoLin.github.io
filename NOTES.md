# Thoughts

## Good AI usage
1. It is well scoped, there is a clear end result that is easily verifiable. 
    1. Bad Example: "Solve computer science" - open ended, no clear result, how would you even verify
    2. Good Example: "Complete monotonous task that I have done a million times before but requires a bit of semantic understanding" - well scoped, clear end result, easy to verify since you have done it before and know what the output should look like.
2. AI tool development needs to be thought of as creating a script. It should be reusable, you shouldn't be inventing new technologies, and writing the script should save you time in the long run
3. AI tools should not replace thinking or magically solve hard problems. You want to learn how to solve the problem yourself, if you use AI to complete a complex task you won't even understand how it completed the task to begin with and you would only be trying to understand enough to GTM. Hard problems create good engineers, taking the AI shortcut will stunt your development and also make you publish giberish

## Heuristics and Principles
Heuristics are good ways to short cut hard decisions when a perfect solution would take either infinite time or there is no clear correct solution
Principles are good to guide you in the right direction when there is high uncertainty. It is like a lighthouse in the fog, it is general guidance and not perfect like GPS. Without principles you will just be aimlessly drifting and you won't tend towards the right direction.
